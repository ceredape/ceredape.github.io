About Me

I have been around computers and games since my early years. After graduating from high school I attended an Electromecanic Engineer university. A few years later, I realized that my heart truly belonged to programming so I changed to a technical programming course. As good as the course was, I discovered the world of video-games development, which made the course look really lacking.

Long story short, I left Argentina to study at ESAT, a well known Spanish game develompemnt school. It was a really challenging three years road, and I'm very proud to say that I got to the end of it. Right after finishing college, I decided return to my country back with my family and friends. A month later I joined Aconcagua Software, where I'm really happy to be at, working as a Unity enigneer.

Currently I am looking forward to keep growing, in a search for challenging projects and passionate people to work with.

Profile

I love creating new features, implementing mechanics, programming the core and rules of games. But I won't lie, solving bugs is something that I consider myself pretty good at.

I have a very good understanding on how to programm solid, reusable, performant peaces of code. I'd rather go a little further with my implementations or when patching someone else's code for the sake of everyone.

When I programm I'm usually a lonely guy. I keep communication open though, always joking around with other programmers/artists, asking question or raising concerns when it's needed.
