About Me

The curiosity for understanding how electronics work took me to apply for an Electromechanical engineering career. In the second year we had some programming courses, I was amazed and hooked by them, so I changed paths. One year later with some good programming foundations my gamer me started to question if I could be a game developer. The course I was in wasn't remotelly close to fulfill that goal, and folliwing tutorials in Youtube felt like I wasn't getting nowhere near to understanding the whole process.

I left Argentina to study at ESAT, a well known Spanish game development school. I've had been promised teachers with real experience in the field, three years of hell and sacrifice, and ending the career as a capable professional game programmer, and boy, they weren't wrong.


Right after finishing college, I decided return to my country back with my family and friends. A month later I joined Aconcagua Software, where I'm really fortunate to be working as a Unity engineer.

Currently I am looking forward to keep growing, in search of challenging projects and passionate people to work with. Complex multiplayer games are of an special interest for me, and I'll head my career in that direction, step by step someday I'll be there.

Profile

I love implementing new features and mechanics, programming the core and rules of games. Solving bugs is something that I consider myself pretty good at. Not a huge fan of engine or graphic programming, but I have a fairly good superficial understanding of those fields.

I believe in:

  • Good programming practices. Reducing code complexity.
  • Respectful communication. Not being afraid of asking questions.
  • Hard work. Never doing the bare minimum a project requires.
  • Not forgetting that kindsness and playfullness it's a need for a healthy team.
